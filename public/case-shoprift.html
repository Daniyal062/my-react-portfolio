01 — The Problem

High Traffic,
Catastrophic Conversion

ShopRift, a boutique sneaker brand, had built a real audience: 40,000 monthly visitors who genuinely cared about their product. But they were converting only 2.1% of those visitors into customers. The industry average for fashion e-commerce is 3.3%. That gap was costing them approximately $18,000/month in unrealized revenue.

Hotjar session recordings revealed that users were scrolling past the Add to Cart button, spending 8+ seconds on product pages without acting, and abandoning specifically at the address entry step. Three distinct drop-off points. Three distinct problems requiring three distinct fixes.

03 — The Three Barriers

One Drop-off,
Three Root Causes

After analyzing session recordings, running 12 user interviews, and reviewing the heatmap data, three distinct conversion barriers emerged. Each had a different root cause and required a different solution.

01
Missing Social Proof

Reviews were buried 400px below the fold. 92% of users never saw them before deciding. The most conversion-driving element in e-commerce was completely invisible at the decision moment.

Solution
Move star rating and review count to directly above the CTA button — adjacent to the ask-for-commitment moment.
02
No Urgency Signal

The site showed no inventory information whatsoever. Users had no reason to act now rather than later. And "later" meant they left and never came back — 74% of abandoned sessions never returned.

Solution
Display honest, real stock counts ("3 left in your size"). Trust-building urgency outperforms manufactured pressure every time.
03
Mobile Form Friction

The checkout form had 6 required fields on mobile. 67% of mobile users abandoned here. For most orders, only name, address, and payment were truly necessary — the rest was friction dressed as data collection.

Solution
Reduce to 3 fields with smart autocomplete. Add Apple Pay and Google Pay as primary options — one tap to purchase.

06 — Results

Conversion Moved.
Significantly.

The redesign was deployed as an A/B test over 3 weeks across 40,000 visitors. Results were statistically significant at 95% confidence. Every change was measured independently to understand which barrier had the most impact.

2.1→3.8%

Overall Conversion

Total conversion rate improved from 2.1% to 3.8% — surpassing the 3.3% industry average and representing ~$28k additional monthly revenue.

–67%

Mobile Abandonment

Checkout abandonment on mobile dropped 67% after reducing the form to 3 fields and adding Apple Pay/Google Pay as primary options.

+41%

CTA Click Rate

Add to Cart click rate increased 41% after moving reviews above the CTA. Evidence adjacent to commitment is the most powerful conversion pattern.

07 — What I Learned

Conversion Design
Changed My Eye

01
Trust converts better than pressure

Removing fake countdown timers and replacing with real stock counts ("3 left in your size") actually increased conversion. Users are sophisticated — they recognize dark patterns and they penalize brands for using them. Honesty is a competitive advantage.

02
Mobile checkout is a different product

Desktop checkout and mobile checkout are not the same form at different sizes. They require fundamentally different information architectures and interaction patterns. The best mobile checkout I designed asks 3 questions, not 6, and lets the phone do the work through Apple/Google Pay.

03
Proximity of evidence to action

Moving reviews from below the fold to above the CTA — a 10-minute Figma change — drove the majority of conversion improvement. Evidence must be adjacent to the moment of commitment. The principle extends everywhere: put the proof next to the ask, always.
